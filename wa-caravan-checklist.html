WA Caravan Pre-Trip Checklist

Built for Western Australian conditions — salt air, red dust, UV, and long-haul outback trips. Tick off every item before you hit the road and after you return. Your van will thank you.

Exterior — Before You Leave
CRITICAL
  • Inspect roof for cracked or lifted seams, bubbles, or delamination
    Water ingress through the roof is the single most expensive caravan repair. Do this first.
    ⚠️ WA — Heat expands and contracts sealant yearly. Check twice a year minimum.
  • Check all roof vent seals and skylight frames
    Press firmly around the frame perimeter — soft spots indicate delamination beneath.
  • Inspect all window and door rubber seals
    Cracked or hardened seals let in water and insects. Replace before they fail, not after.
    ⚠️ WA — UV dries out rubber seals 2–3× faster than east coast states.
  • Check slide-out seals for debris, tears or mould
    Clean with a soft brush and apply slide-out lubricant if stiff or sticky.
  • Inspect exterior paint/gelcoat for oxidation, fading or chalking
    Run your hand across the surface — white residue on your palm means active oxidation. Treat before it etches deeper.
    ⚠️ WA — North-facing panels oxidise fastest. Check those first.
  • Check for black streaks from roof run-off
    Black streaks are algae and grime. Left untreated they stain permanently. Remove with a dedicated streak remover.
  • Check awning fabric for mould, tears or broken fittings
    Roll out fully and inspect. Never pack away a wet awning — guaranteed mould.
  • Inspect exterior lights — brake, reverse, indicators, marker lights
    Check the lenses for cracks. Moisture inside a light housing corrodes the connection.
  • Check stone guards, checker plate, and underbody protection
    Gravel rash chips on aluminium and fibreglass allow moisture and corrosion to start underneath.
    ⚠️ WA — Red Kimberley and Pilbara gravel is particularly abrasive.
  • Check and grease all external hinges, locks and drawer catches
    Salt air accelerates corrosion on hardware. A wipe of lanolin-based lubricant prevents this.
🛞
Tyres & Running Gear
CRITICAL
  • Check tyre pressure on all tyres including spare
    Check when cold. Refer to van door placard for correct pressures — not the tyre sidewall.
    ⚠️ WA — Tyre pressure rises 4–6 psi in summer heat. Check before dawn if possible.
  • Inspect tyres for sidewall cracking, bulges, or uneven wear
    Caravans sit stationary for long periods — UV and ozone crack sidewalls even when tread depth is fine. Age matters as much as wear.
  • Check wheel nuts are torqued correctly after first 100km
    New or recently removed wheels settle. Re-torque after the first day of travel.
  • Inspect wheel bearings (listen for grinding or play when jacking)
    Failing bearings are a common cause of caravan fires. Inspect annually or every 10,000km.
  • Check breakaway cable is connected and battery charged
    Required by law in WA. Test the battery annually — they go flat in storage.
  • Inspect jockey wheel and A-frame for corrosion or damage
🛋️
Interior — Before You Leave
IMPORTANT
  • Check for moisture or mould in cupboards and under seats
    Small leaks show up as musty smells or staining. Find the source before it spreads.
  • Test all appliances — fridge, hot water, stove
    Run the fridge 24 hours before departure to reach temperature.
  • Check gas lines and connections for leaks
    Brush soapy water on connections — bubbles mean a leak. Don't use a naked flame to check.
  • Test smoke and CO detectors
    Replace batteries annually. CO detectors have a 5-year lifespan.
  • Check water pump and fresh water tank
    Flush the tank with a water sanitiser if it's been sitting for more than a month.
    ⚠️ WA — Bacterial growth accelerates in summer heat. Sanitise more frequently.
  • Check toilet cassette is clean and chemical levels correct
  • Test 12V electrics — lights, USB, fans
  • Check solar panel output and battery bank levels
    Clean panels lose up to 25% efficiency. Dust and bird droppings are the main culprits.
🏁
After Every Trip — Post-Trip Checklist
IMPORTANT
  • Rinse exterior thoroughly — especially undercarriage and wheel arches
    Salt air and road grime accelerate corrosion. Rinse within 48 hours of a coastal trip.
    ⚠️ WA — Red Kimberley mud sets like concrete if left to dry. Rinse immediately.
  • Clean awning before rolling up — rinse and allow to dry fully
    Rolling up a damp awning with salt, dust or bird droppings guarantees mould and staining.
  • Remove all food and perishables from fridge and pantry
  • Flush and sanitise fresh water system
    Don't leave water sitting in tanks between trips — especially in WA summers.
  • Empty, clean and dry toilet cassette
  • Vacuum and mop interior — pay attention to entry areas and kitchen
  • Check roof and seals for new damage from the trip
    Overhanging branches, low bridge markers and caravan park trees are the most common culprits.
  • Record any issues in a maintenance log
    Small problems get forgotten. A simple note in your phone prevents expensive surprises.
🏠
Putting the Van into Storage
SEASONAL
  • Book a professional detail before storage
    Salt, grime and oxidation continue to work on the surface during storage. A full detail with UV sealant stops this.
  • Apply UV protectant to all exterior rubber seals
    A silicone-based seal conditioner prevents cracking over summer.
  • Place tyre covers on all wheels
    UV destroys sidewalls faster than miles. Essential for outdoor storage.
  • Disconnect battery or connect to a maintenance charger
  • Cover vents and openings to prevent insect/rodent entry
    Mesh covers over vents and fly screens left closed are the simplest prevention.
  • Leave interior cupboards open slightly for airflow
    Prevents musty odours and mould in confined spaces during long storage.
  • Document the van's condition with photos before storage
    Helps identify any new damage when you return, and is useful for insurance.
  • Check insurance policy is current and covers storage period
⚓
Boat Trailer — If You Also Tow a Boat
BONUS
  • Rinse trailer frame thoroughly after every boat ramp use
    Salt water immersion at every launch is the fastest path to galvanic corrosion. Rinse within 2 hours.
    ⚠️ WA — Mandurah, Hillarys and Fremantle ramps are all high-salinity environments.
  • Inspect frame for rust spots, bubbling paint or white corrosion
    Treat rust spots with converter before they perforate the galvanising — early treatment costs nothing; ignored rust costs a new trailer.
  • Grease wheel bearings or check bearing buddies
    Salt water flushes out grease on every submersion. Failed bearings are the most common cause of trailer accidents in WA.
  • Check trailer lighting — submersion contacts corrode quickly
    Apply dielectric grease to all connectors after rinsing. Not optional in WA's salt environment.
  • Inspect bunks or rollers for wear, cracking or looseness
    Worn bunks allow the hull to rest on metal — causing stress cracks in fibreglass over time.
  • Apply anti-corrosion wax or lanolin spray to frame annually
    One professional anti-corrosion treatment per year significantly extends trailer life in WA.
