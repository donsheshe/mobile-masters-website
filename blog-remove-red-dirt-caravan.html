How to Remove Red Dirt From Your Caravan After a WA Trip

If you've just returned from the Pilbara, the Kimberley, the Gibb River Road or the Nullarbor, you'll know the problem: a fine red film over every panel, baked into the seals, and worked deep into the awning and checkerplate. Western Australian red dirt is not ordinary dust — and treating it like ordinary dust is how finishes get ruined.

Why WA Red Dirt Is Different

WA red dirt is rich in iron oxide. Under Perth's UV and heat, those fine iron particles don't just sit on the surface — they bond to gelcoat, fibreglass and rubber. Unlike normal dust that rinses away, iron oxide chemically adheres, which is why a van can still look stained after a thorough wash.

Why a Normal Wash Isn't Enough

A pressure wash removes the loose surface layer, but the embedded particles remain. Worse, scrubbing dry or semi-dry red dirt drags those abrasive particles across the surface, causing micro-scratches that permanently dull fibreglass. The fix isn't more elbow grease — it's the right chemistry.

The Right Way to Remove It — Step by Step

  • 1. Gentle rinse first. Flood the surface with water to float off as much loose dirt as possible before touching it.
  • 2. Iron decontamination wash. A specialist iron-fallout remover chemically dissolves the bonded iron particles (it visibly turns purple as it works). This is the step a normal wash skips.
  • 3. Clay bar. A clay bar physically lifts any remaining bonded contaminants, leaving the surface glass-smooth.
  • 4. Polish if needed. Inspect under light; machine-polish any remaining staining or oxidation to restore gloss.
  • 5. Seal or coat. A UV-resistant sealant or ceramic coating protects against future contamination and makes the next clean far easier.
  • 6. Don't forget the interior. Red dirt gets inside through every vent and seal. Fabrics, carpet and storage areas need proper extraction — not just a vacuum.

When to Call a Professional

Light dust after a short trip you can manage yourself. But a heavily contaminated van — especially one that's been sitting in the sun for a couple of weeks — needs iron decontamination and clay treatment to come up properly without scratching. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I just pressure wash red dirt off?+
A pressure wash removes loose surface dirt but not the iron particles bonded to the surface. Those need chemical iron decontamination to release safely — otherwise staining and micro-scratching remain.
Does red dirt stain permanently?+
It can if left untreated in the sun, because bonded iron oxide etches into gelcoat over time. Treated promptly with the right products, it removes cleanly.
How long does professional red dirt removal take?+
Typically 3–6 hours for a caravan depending on contamination level and size. Vans brought in soon after a trip are faster than ones left to bake for weeks.
